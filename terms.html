3. What FitStakes is (and isn't)

FitStakes is a fitness accountability platform based on commitment contracts. You pledge your own money toward your own goal. It is not gambling and not a wager: there is no prize pool, no wagering against other users, and no element of chance. The only outcome is that you keep your money (on success) or your pledge is donated to a charity you selected (on failure). We do not hold or pool user funds.

4. How a stake works

  • Set-up: You connect a payment method and we create a payment-authorization mandate with our processor (Stripe). No charge is made at this time.
  • Verification: Progress is determined only from device/sensor data you connect (Apple Health, Android Health Connect, or Strava). Manually entered activity does not count.
  • Success: If you meet your goal, you are charged nothing and the obligation is released.
  • Failure: If you do not meet your goal, you authorize us to charge your pledged amount off-session and route it as a donation to the charity you chose, through a third-party charitable-disbursement provider that acts as donor of record and issues any receipt.

5. Fees

The pledge amount you see is inclusive of fees. On a forfeit, a platform fee (currently 3.5%–5%) plus payment-processing fees are deducted from the pledged amount, and the charity receives the remainder. The exact fee is disclosed to you before you commit. Corporate matching donations are disbursed in full with no platform fee. See the Fee Disclosure.

6. Nature of a forfeit; no refunds

A forfeited pledge is a contractual consequence of not meeting your goal, not a purchase of goods or services and not a voluntary charitable gift by you. Forfeits are non-refundable and, for individuals, are not a personal tax-deductible donation.

7. Payment authorization

You authorize us and our processor to charge your saved payment method the pledged amount if you fail your goal, without further action by you (an "off-session" charge). If a charge is declined or requires authentication, we may retry, update card details via the card networks, and contact you to complete payment. Unpaid forfeits may be treated as amounts owed.

8. Charities

Charities are drawn from verified U.S. tax-exempt organizations, checked for exempt, deductible, and not-revoked status before selection. The disbursement provider is the legal donor of record for routed forfeits.
